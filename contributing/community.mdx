LLVM is a large, distributed open source project with an active community across multiple communication channels. The [LLVM Code of Conduct](https://llvm.org/docs/CodeOfConduct.html) applies to all of them.

## Discourse forums

The [LLVM Discourse forums](https://discourse.llvm.org) are the primary communication channel for the project. Discourse is the successor to the former mailing lists (`llvm-dev@`, `cfe-dev@`, `lldb-dev@`, etc.).

Use Discourse for:

- Proposing major changes or new designs as RFCs (Requests for Comment)
- Asking questions about LLVM internals or development practices
- Announcements, release notes, and project-wide discussions
- Subproject-specific discussions in the relevant category

Discourse is public and archived. Confidentiality or non-disclosure notices cannot be respected on this platform.

<Note>
The [Announcements category](https://discourse.llvm.org/c/announce/46) covers project-wide announcements such as releases, developer meetings, and blog posts.
</Note>

## Discord

The [LLVM Discord server](https://discord.gg/xS7Z362) provides real-time chat for users and developers of LLVM and its subprojects (including Clang). The server is actively moderated.

Notable channels include:

- **#buildbot-status** — automated notifications when LLVM buildbots change state (passing to failing or back)
- **#office-hours** — announcements from contributors hosting office hours sessions
- **#beginners** — a welcoming space for new contributors

The Discord bot also supports `@mention` notifications when your email address appears on a buildbot blamelist. DM `help` to the bot for details.

## Online sync-ups and working groups

LLVM organizes regular video calls on specific topics. These are open to anyone interested. The [LLVM community calendar](https://calendar.google.com/calendar/u/0/embed?src=calendar@llvm.org) lists all upcoming events.

Current active sync-ups include:

- **Loop Optimization Working Group** — first Wednesday of the month
- **RISC-V** — every two weeks on Thursday
- **LLVM Security Group** — monthly, every third Tuesday
- **Clang C and C++ Language Working Group** — 1st and 3rd Wednesday of the month
- **GlobalISel** — every second Tuesday of the month
- **MLIR C/C++ Frontend Working Group** — monthly, usually first Monday
- **Vectorizer Improvement Working Group** — third Tuesday of the month

The Code of Conduct applies to all online sync-ups.

## Mailing lists

Several commit and bug mailing lists are available for those who want to track LLVM activity via email:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="llvm-commits">
    Receives all commit messages when LLVM developers push changes to the repository. Very high volume. Archives available at [lists.llvm.org](http://lists.llvm.org/pipermail/llvm-commits/).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="llvm-bugs">
    Receives a notification every time a bug is opened or closed on the issue tracker. Higher volume than the general discussion forum. Archives at [lists.llvm.org](http://lists.llvm.org/pipermail/llvm-bugs/).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

You can also subscribe to GitHub team notifications for specific parts of the codebase:

- [pr-subscribers-\*](https://github.com/orgs/llvm/teams?query=pr-subscribers) teams — notified of pull requests touching specific paths
- [issue-subscribers-\*](https://github.com/orgs/llvm/teams?query=issue-subscribers) teams — notified of issues in specific components

## LLVM Developer Conferences

LLVM holds developer meetings and conferences where contributors present talks, tutorials, and technical discussions. Past and upcoming events are listed at [llvm.org/devmtg](https://llvm.org/devmtg/).

There are also local user groups called [LLVM Socials](https://www.meetup.com/pro/llvm/) in cities around the world. If there is no group in your city, you can start one — see the [Meetup Guidelines](https://llvm.org/docs/MeetupGuidelines.html).

## Code of Conduct

The LLVM Community [Code of Conduct](https://llvm.org/docs/CodeOfConduct.html) applies to all participants in all modes of communication: Discourse forums, Discord, GitHub, office hours, online sync-ups, and developer conferences.

To report a Code of Conduct issue, contact the event organizers and email [conduct@llvm.org](mailto:conduct@llvm.org).
